Research Mission

Our research focuses on developing, assessing, and implementing patient-centered AI solutions that ease healthcare delivery and improve patient outcomes. Using expertise in NLP, machine learning, and biostatistics, we analyze the full patient journey to uncover insights from patient–doctor communication while contributing to AI governance and ethical policy development in healthcare.

Research

Our work focuses on developing and evaluating methods that use routinely collected health data to generate evidence and assess AI systems in clinical settings. We combine methodological development with applied studies, with an emphasis on validity, fairness, and real-world performance.

Research Areas

  • Clinical NLP and patient-generated data
    We study how language data—clinical notes, voice recordings, and patient-reported data—can be used for measurement and prediction. Our work focuses on extracting meaningful signals while accounting for noise, variability, and context.
  • Real-world evidence from EHRs
    We develop approaches to use observational health data for study design and inference. This includes handling missing data, bias, and heterogeneity in large-scale EHR datasets.
  • Evaluation of AI in clinical practice
    We study how AI systems perform when deployed in real-world settings. Our work focuses on evaluation frameworks that go beyond accuracy to assess impact on clinical decisions and outcomes.
  • AI ethics, fairness, and governance
    We examine how AI systems affect equity and decision-making in healthcare. This includes studying bias, fairness, and how AI tools are integrated into clinical workflows.
  • Decision support in serious illness and palliative care
    We develop and evaluate AI approaches to support complex clinical decision-making in serious illness, with a focus on communication, uncertainty, and patient-centered care.

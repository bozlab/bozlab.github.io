About

The Boz Lab is a research group in the Department of Biomedical Informatics at Emory University, directed by Selen Bozkurt, PhD, FAMIA, Assistant Professor. The lab develops computational methods for study design, measurement, and inference using real-world data, with emphasis on unstructured and patient-generated data and on the evaluation of AI systems.

Mission & Values

  • Rigor: Methods-driven research emphasizing study design and measurement validity
  • Responsibility: Transparent, reproducible, and ethical AI evaluation
  • Real-world focus: Evidence that translates to practice and informs policy

Research Areas

Real-World Evidence

Methods for generating valid, reproducible evidence from observational data. Focus on study design, confounding adjustment, and causal inference using electronic health records.

Unstructured & Patient-Generated Data

Measurement of clinical and behavioral constructs from narrative text, EHRs, and patient-reported outcomes. Validation of NLP and LLM-extracted features.

AI Evaluation

Rigorous assessment of machine learning system performance in real-world settings. Generalizability, bias assessment, and evaluation of fairness across populations.

Reproducibility & Reporting

Standards and frameworks for transparent, reproducible methods and reporting. Development of guidance documents and evaluation frameworks.

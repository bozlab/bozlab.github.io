AI and data science for real-world evidence

AI for real-world evidence, and real-world evidence for AI.

Hi. I am an Assistant Professor of Biomedical Informatics at Emory University. I lead Boz Lab, which develops computational methods for study design, measurement, and inference using real-world data, with emphasis on unstructured and patient-generated data and on the evaluation of AI systems.

My research draws on training in statistics, biostatistics, and medical informatics, and spans real-world evidence generation, natural language processing, large language models, and responsible AI. The lab's work is motivated by a simple question: how can routine data be used more rigorously to generate evidence, and how can those same data be used to evaluate AI in practice?

Research

The lab focuses on four related areas:

  • Real-world evidence
  • Unstructured and patient-generated data
  • Evaluation of AI
  • Reproducibility and reporting

Across these areas, we are interested in validity, reproducibility, bias assessment, and the responsible use of computational methods in health research.

Boz Lab

AI and data science for real-world evidence

AI for real-world evidence, and real-world evidence for AI

The lab develops computational methods for study design, measurement, and inference using real-world data, with emphasis on unstructured and patient-generated data and on the evaluation of AI systems.

Current Projects

  • NIMH R21 (2024–2026): Digital phenotyping of electronic health records for loneliness and suicide risk
  • Sanofi IDEAtech Award (2025–2026): AI for clinical trial diversity using real-world data
  • Fraser-Parker Foundation (2025–2026): AI for patient-reported symptom analysis

Research

  • Real-world evidence: Methods for generating valid, reproducible evidence from observational data
  • Unstructured and patient-generated data: Measurement of constructs from clinical text, EHRs, and patient reports
  • Evaluation of AI: Generalizability, bias assessment, and real-world performance of machine learning systems
  • Reproducibility and reporting: Transparency and standards for methods and findings
